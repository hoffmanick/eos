1 Introduction

As part of Neotoma’s participation in the Ethical Open Science for Past Global Change Data RCN, we are auditing our records for sensitive data, especially data which are sensitive with respect to the CARE principles.

The main audit document is based on programmatic searches of Neotoma metadata. These structured metadata are curated by a community of experts, so they’re a highly accurate source for making determinations about Neotoma’s holdings. However, they are necessarily limited in scope. This page documents our attempt to extend our audit through programmatic searches of the full texts of publications associated with datasets in Neotoma according to Neotoma’s metadata. This corpus is more extensive, but it is harder to draw conclusions from our hits because it is unstructured.

2 Neotoma Publications Indexed by XDD

Neotoma contains 14192 records. Of these 14192 records, only 3137 have DOIs. 6 of the DOIs are problematically formatted. Of the 3131 (22.1 %) articles with unproblematic DOIs, I searched for how many had internal document ids (XDD ids), by looping all the DOIs through the API call: https://xdd.wisc.edu/api/articles?doi=. Only 1381 (9.7 % ) publications had an internal ID with XDD. It seems likely (should verify this later) that only publications with a an internal XDD id are indexed by XDD. However, I conduct the following searches using DOI, not internal XDD ID, just in case…

4 Radiocarbon from Human Samples

As part of our interest in our repository’s relationship to CARE, we want to know about the disposition of the remains of human ancestors, for both radiocarbon dates and samples. There are 44619 records in Neotoma’s geochronology table, of which there are 7000 records ambiguous with respect to taxon (meaning the materialdated field is bone collagen, bone, blank, or NA). We would like to search for those ambiguous taxa which are most likely to be dated from human material.

We searched for all the publications in Neotoma associated with these ambiguous records and found 897 records. Of these records, only 209 have an unproblematic DOI, and 45 have an internal XDD ID.
